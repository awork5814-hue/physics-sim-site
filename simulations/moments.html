Moments & Equilibrium

The turning effect of forces

📚 Theory

A moment (torque) is the turning effect of a force: M = F × d, where d is the perpendicular distance from the pivot.

For equilibrium: ΣF = 0 and ΣM = 0.

🔬 Investigation Questions
  1. Place equal masses at equal distances. Is it balanced?
  2. Double the distance, halve the mass. Is it still balanced?
  3. Move the fulcrum closer to the heavier mass. What happens?
💡 Key Takeaways
  • Moment = Force × Distance.
  • Equilibrium requires clockwise moments = anticlockwise moments.
  • Longer distance increases turning effect.
