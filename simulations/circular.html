What is Uniform Circular Motion?

An object moving in a circle at constant speed. Although speed is constant, velocity changes because direction changes continuously.

This means there is always an acceleration directed toward the center of the circle, called centripetal acceleration.

Key Formulas

v = ωr
Speed equals angular velocity times radius
a = v²/r = ω²r
Centripetal acceleration
F = mω²r = mv²/r
Centripetal force (net inward force)
T = 2π/ω
Period (time for one complete revolution)

Vector Diagram

Velocity (v): tangent to the circle
Acceleration (a): points to center
Force (F): same direction as acceleration
