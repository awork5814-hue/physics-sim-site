📚 Theory

A simple pendulum is a mass (bob) attached to a fixed pivot by a massless string. For small angles, the period depends only on length and gravity.

Formula: T = 2π√(L/g)

Longer length means a longer period, and stronger gravity means a shorter period. Mass does not affect the period.

🔬 Investigation Questions
  1. Keep g = 9.81 and θ₀ = 30°. Change L from 0.5 m to 2 m. What happens to the period?
  2. Keep L = 1 m. Compare the period on Earth vs Moon. Which is longer and why?
  3. Change mass from 0.5 kg to 5 kg with same L and g. Does period change?
  4. Small angle (10°) vs large angle (60°): Is the formula accurate for both?
💡 Key Takeaways
  • Period formula: T = 2π√(L/g)
  • Independent of mass for small angles.
  • Useful for measuring gravity.
  • Best accuracy for angles below ~15°.
