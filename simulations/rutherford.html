Rutherford Gold Foil Experiment

Discovery of the atomic nucleus

📚 Theory

Most alpha particles passed through the foil, but a few deflected at large angles. This showed that atoms have a tiny, dense, positively charged nucleus.

🔬 Investigation Questions
  1. Why do most particles pass straight through?
  2. What causes rare large-angle deflections?
  3. How does Z affect scattering?
💡 Key Takeaways
  • Atoms are mostly empty space.
  • Positive charge is concentrated in the nucleus.
  • Large Z yields stronger deflection.
