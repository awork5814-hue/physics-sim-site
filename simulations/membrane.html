What you’re seeing: The phospholipid bilayer is a selective barrier. Sodium (Na⁺) is higher outside and potassium (K⁺) higher inside. Diffusion lets ions drift down their gradients, while the Na⁺/K⁺ pump uses ATP to move ions against the gradient, maintaining the membrane potential.
