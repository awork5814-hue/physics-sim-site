Ohm's Law

The relationship between Voltage (V), Current (I), and Resistance (R)

Voltage 0.0 V
Current 0.00 A
Resistance 1 Ω
Power 0.00 W
V = I × R
I = V / R
R = V / I
📚 Theory

Ohm's Law states that the current through a conductor between two points is directly proportional to the voltage across those two points and inversely proportional to the resistance.

Voltage is the "push" that drives charges, current is the flow of charges, and resistance is how strongly the circuit opposes that flow.

Optional analogy: Think of voltage as water pressure, current as the flow rate, and resistance as the width of the pipe.

💡 Key Takeaways
  • Voltage and current are directly proportional when resistance is constant.
  • Current decreases as resistance increases.
  • Power scales with voltage and current: P = V × I.
