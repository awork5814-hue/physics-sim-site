Boyle's Law

Pressure × Volume = Constant (isothermal)

P–V Diagram

P ∝ 1/V
📚 Theory

Boyle's Law states that for a fixed amount of gas at constant temperature, pressure is inversely proportional to volume: P × V = constant.

When volume decreases, molecules collide more frequently with the walls, raising pressure.

🔬 Investigation Questions
  1. Double the pressure. What happens to volume?
  2. Why does a syringe get harder to push as you compress it?
  3. Calculate P₁ × V₁ and P₂ × V₂. Are they equal?
💡 Key Takeaways
  • Inverse relationship: P ∝ 1/V.
  • P–V graph is a hyperbola.
  • Temperature must be constant for Boyle's Law.
