Wave Motion

Transverse & Longitudinal Waves • v = fλ

Displacement vs Time

Single point motion
📚 Theory

A wave is a disturbance that transfers energy without transporting matter. Particles oscillate around fixed positions while the wave travels.

Key relation: v = f × λ

🔬 Investigation Questions
  1. Keep λ constant and double f. What happens to wave speed?
  2. Keep f constant and double λ. What happens to wave speed?
  3. Watch a single particle. Does it move with the wave?
💡 Key Takeaways
  • Waves transport energy, not matter.
  • Transverse: perpendicular motion. Longitudinal: parallel motion.
  • v = fλ relates speed, frequency, wavelength.
  • Period: T = 1/f.
