MOSFET Operation

The MOSFET (Metal-Oxide-Semiconductor Field-Effect Transistor) is a voltage-controlled device:

  • Gate: Controls channel formation
  • Source: Where carriers enter
  • Drain: Where carriers exit

When VGS > Vt, a channel forms and current flows from drain to source.

BJT Operation

The BJT (Bipolar Junction Transistor) is a current-controlled device:

  • Base: Controls current flow
  • Emitter: Emits carriers
  • Collector: Collects carriers

IC = β × IB. A small base current controls a larger collector current.

Operating Regions
  • Cutoff: No current flows (device OFF)
  • Triode/Linear: Current proportional to VDS (amplifying)
  • Saturation: Maximum current (device fully ON)
