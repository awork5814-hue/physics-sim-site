Series vs Parallel Circuits

Understanding current, voltage, and resistance in two classic configurations

Series Circuit Series
Parallel Circuit Parallel

Comparison Summary

Property Series Parallel
Current Same everywhere Splits at junction
Voltage Divides across resistors Same across each resistor
Resistance R = R1 + R2 1/R = 1/R1 + 1/R2
Brightness (bulbs) Dimmer Brighter
📚 Theory Cards

Series Circuit: Current is the same through all components, voltage divides across resistors, and total resistance is the sum of individual resistances.

Parallel Circuit: Voltage is the same across all branches, current splits at junctions, and total resistance is always less than the smallest branch.

🧮 Formula Reference

Series: R_total = R1 + R2 + R3 + ...

Parallel: 1/R_total = 1/R1 + 1/R2 + 1/R3 + ...

Two branches: R_total = (R1 × R2) / (R1 + R2)

🔬 Guided Investigation
  1. Set both circuits to the same R values. What happens to brightness when bulbs are in parallel vs series?
  2. Keep V constant, increase R2 in parallel. Does R1's current change?
  3. Keep V constant, increase R2 in series. How does total current change?
  4. Calculate: If V = 12V, R1 = 100Ω, R2 = 100Ω in parallel, what is total current?
💡 Key Takeaways
  • Series: current conservation and voltage division.
  • Parallel: voltage conservation and current division.
  • Real-world wiring uses parallel so devices work independently.
