SHM Formulas

a = -ω²x
ω = √(k/m) (mass-spring), ω = √(g/L) (pendulum, small angles)
T = 2π/ω
v = -Aω sin(ωt + φ), x = A cos(ωt + φ)

📚 Theory

Simple harmonic motion is periodic motion where acceleration is proportional and opposite to displacement: a = -ω²x.

🔬 Investigation Questions
  1. Double the mass in a spring system. What happens to the period?
  2. At maximum displacement, what is the kinetic energy?
  3. When x is zero, what is v and a?
💡 Key Takeaways
  • SHM: a = -ω²x.
  • Mass-spring: T = 2π√(m/k).
  • Pendulum: T = 2π√(L/g) (small angles).
  • Energy oscillates between KE and PE.
