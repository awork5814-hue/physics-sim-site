Specific Heat Capacity

Heat required to change temperature: Q = mcΔT

Temperature vs Time

Heating curve
📚 Theory

Specific heat capacity is the energy needed to raise 1 kg of a substance by 1°C. Water has a very high specific heat, so it changes temperature slowly.

Formula: Q = m × c × ΔT

🔬 Investigation Questions
  1. Heat 1 kg of water vs 1 kg of copper by 50°C. Which needs more energy?
  2. Double the mass of water. How much more heat is needed?
  3. Why does a full kettle take longer than a half-full one?
💡 Key Takeaways
  • Q = mcΔT governs heating and cooling.
  • High c → slower temperature change.
  • Water moderates climate due to high c.
