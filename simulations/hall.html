Hall Effect

When a current-carrying conductor is placed in a perpendicular magnetic field, a voltage develops across the conductor - this is the Hall Effect.

Key Physics:

• Electrons deflect due to Lorentz force F = qv×B

• This creates a transverse Hall voltage

• The Hall voltage polarity reveals carrier type (+ for holes, - for electrons)

• Used in sensors, car ignition, and semiconductor analysis
