Acceleration

Rate of change of velocity — explore motion dynamics

Motion Graphs

s(t) v(t) a(t)
📚 Theory

Acceleration is the rate of change of velocity with respect to time. When acceleration is constant, the velocity changes uniformly, producing a linear v-t graph and a parabolic s-t graph.

The equations of motion for constant acceleration are:

  • v = u + at (velocity-time)
  • s = ut + ½at² (position-time)
  • v² = u² + 2as (velocity-displacement)

💡 Key Takeaways
  • Acceleration: a = Δv/Δt (rate of velocity change)
  • Constant a → linear v-t graph, parabolic s-t graph
  • Area under v-t gives displacement
  • Slope of v-t gives acceleration
