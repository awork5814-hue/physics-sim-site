Cyclotron

A cyclotron uses a constant magnetic field and alternating electric field to accelerate particles in a spiral path.

  • Frequency: f = qB/2πm (constant)
  • Radius: r = mv/qB
  • Energy: E = q²B²r²/2m
Synchrotron

A synchrotron uses varying magnetic fields and RF to keep particles in a fixed-radius orbit as they accelerate.

  • Orbit: Constant radius
  • B-field: Increases with energy
  • RF: Synchronized to particle
Linear Accelerator (Linac)

A linac accelerates particles in a straight line through a series of accelerating electrodes.

  • Structure: Drift tubes
  • Advantage: No synchrotron radiation
  • Use: Electron accelerators
