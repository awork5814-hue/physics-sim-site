Refraction & Lenses

Snell's Law • Total Internal Reflection • Image Formation

Try switching materials to see critical angle and TIR used in fiber optics. For lenses, adjust object distance to create real or virtual images.

📚 Theory

Refraction occurs when light changes speed as it moves between media. Snell's Law relates the angles and refractive indices: n₁ sin θ₁ = n₂ sin θ₂.

Total internal reflection happens when light goes from a higher index to a lower index and the angle exceeds the critical angle.

🔬 Investigation Questions
  1. When light goes from air to glass, does it bend toward or away from the normal?
  2. What happens when θ₁ exceeds the critical angle?
  3. Move the object closer than the focal length. What type of image is formed?
💡 Key Takeaways
  • n₁ < n₂: bends toward the normal.
  • n₁ > n₂: bends away from the normal.
  • Total internal reflection enables fiber optics.
  • Lens images depend on focal length and object distance.
