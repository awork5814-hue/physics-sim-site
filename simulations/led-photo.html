LED & Photodiodes

LED (Light Emitting Diode): When forward-biased, electrons recombine with holes at the p-n junction, releasing photons. Higher current = more light.

Photodiode: When reverse-biased and exposed to light, photon absorption creates electron-hole pairs, generating photocurrent.

Key Formulas:

• Photon Energy: E(eV) = 1240/λ(nm)

• Forward Voltage: Vf ≈ Eg (band gap)

• Responsivity: R = ηqλ/hc
