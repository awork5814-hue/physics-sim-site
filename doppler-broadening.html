Physics Explained

The Doppler Effect

Atoms moving toward the observer appear to emit at higher frequencies (blue shift), while receding atoms emit at lower frequencies (red shift). This creates a range of observed wavelengths.

Gaussian Profile

The thermal velocity distribution follows Maxwell-Boltzmann statistics, resulting in a Gaussian spectral line shape: I(ν) = I₀ · exp(-(ν-ν₀)²/2σ²)

σ = (ν₀/c) · √(2kT/m)

Temperature Dependence

Higher temperatures → faster atoms → broader spectral lines. The FWHM scales with the square root of temperature: FWHM ∝ √T

Mass Dependence

Heavier atoms move slower at the same temperature, producing narrower lines. This is why hydrogen lines are broader than iron lines at the same temperature.
